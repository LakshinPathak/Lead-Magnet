The real problem

Six ways B2B marketing
teams quietly bleed revenue

None of these show up on a dashboard. They compound silently until a board meeting forces the conversation.

01
📭
The pipeline that looks healthy but isn't
MQLs are being logged, demos are being booked, yet revenue doesn't move. The top of funnel is technically active but attracting the wrong companies at the wrong stage.
Affects 67% of teams
02
🪣
Buying intent that disappears into silence
A prospect downloads a guide, attends a webinar, then gets nothing for three weeks. By the time sales follows up, they've already signed with a competitor who stayed in touch.
80% of leads wasted here
03
🙈
Budget going to channels that feel productive
Without proper attribution, spend follows activity rather than results. The LinkedIn posts with high impressions get more budget. The email sequence that actually closes deals gets defunded.
Affects 78% of teams
04
🌫️
Content that speaks to everyone and converts nobody
When the ICP is not locked down to specific firmographics and buying triggers, content becomes a generic blog strategy. It attracts traffic from people who will never pay you a pound.
Top conversion killer
05
✍️
A content operation with no measurable return
Articles, case studies, LinkedIn posts go out every week. Nobody can tell you which piece influenced the last five deals. The budget request next quarter will be a fight you can't win.
Fixable in 30 days
06
🐢
Deals that stall three weeks before signing
The deal is verbally agreed. Then procurement, legal, and a third stakeholder appear from nowhere. Marketing has no content or process to help sales keep momentum through this phase.
High revenue risk

Questions

Things people ask
before running it

Who is this built for exactly? +
GrowthDiag is for marketing leaders at B2B agencies, SaaS companies, and professional services firms. That includes CMOs, VPs of Marketing, Heads of Growth, and founders who run marketing themselves. It works best for companies with at least 500K in annual revenue that are trying to build a repeatable pipeline rather than relying on referrals and founder relationships.
Is it actually free or is there a catch? +
It is free. No credit card. No trial period that converts to a paid subscription. We ask for your name and work email before showing the full report. We use that to send you a follow-up email with the report summary and an optional link to book a free strategy call. You can unsubscribe from anything in one click. We do not sell your contact details to third parties.
How accurate is the AI analysis? +
The accuracy depends on the honesty of your answers. If you pick the option that sounds best rather than the one that reflects reality, the output will be less useful. The AI is built on Claude by Anthropic and has been tested across more than 500 companies. The diagnostic categories are based on real B2B marketing frameworks. That said, it is a starting point for strategic thinking, not a replacement for deep analysis of your specific business data.
How long does it take? +
Eight questions with four options each. Most people finish in two to three minutes. There is no open text input so nothing slows you down. The AI analysis runs in the background while you enter your contact details and the result appears within a few seconds of submitting the form.
What exactly do I get at the end? +
You receive a pipeline health score out of 100 benchmarked against 500+ companies, your score gap versus the industry average, identification of your top three revenue bottlenecks with severity ratings and root cause explanations, a four-phase 90-day roadmap with specific weekly tasks, six target outcome metrics to track your progress, and a link to book an optional free 30-minute strategy call with our team.
Can I use this with my team? +
Yes and we recommend it. Several CMOs have told us they use the diagnostic as a team alignment exercise. Each person on the marketing leadership team completes it independently, then the group compares scores to surface disagreements about where the marketing engine actually stands. The differences between answers often reveal more than the answers themselves.
