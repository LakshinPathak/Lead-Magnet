FAQ

Common questions ❓

Who is this diagnostic built for? +
GrowthDiag is built for marketing leaders at B2B agencies, SaaS companies, and professional services firms — including CMOs, VPs of Marketing, Heads of Growth, and Founders managing their own marketing function. It works best for companies with at least £500K ARR that are trying to build or scale a repeatable pipeline.
Is it really free? What's the catch? +
Yes, completely free. No credit card. No trial period. We gate the full report behind a name and work email because we use it to follow up with a genuinely useful email and an optional booking link for a free strategy call. We don't sell your data and you can unsubscribe in one click.
How accurate is the AI analysis? +
The quality of your output depends on the honesty of your inputs. The AI is powered by Claude (Anthropic) and is prompted with B2B marketing strategy expertise. It has been tested across 500+ companies and the diagnostic categories are based on real frameworks used by senior B2B marketers. That said, it's a starting point — not a replacement for deep strategic work.
How long does it take to complete? +
The quiz has 8 multiple-choice questions and takes approximately 3 minutes to complete. There's no right or wrong answer — the goal is to capture where you actually are, not where you'd like to be.
What do I get at the end? +
You receive: (1) a pipeline health score out of 100 benchmarked against 500+ B2B companies, (2) a gap vs. industry average, (3) your top 3 revenue bottlenecks with severity ratings and root-cause explanations, (4) a 4-step 90-day fix roadmap with specific actions, (5) 6 target outcome metrics to track success, and (6) an optional booking link for a free 30-minute strategy call.
Can I share the results with my team? +
Yes. Your results page can be printed, screenshot, or PDF'd. We're also working on a share link feature. Many CMOs use the diagnostic as a team alignment exercise — everyone answers individually and then compares scores to surface disagreements about where the marketing engine actually stands.
